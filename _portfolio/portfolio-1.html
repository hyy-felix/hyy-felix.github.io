Key Results

  • Developed a multi-mode platform capable of driving and flying
  • Achieved autonomous navigation and control in both modes
  • Demonstrated safe and efficient transition between driving and flying

Platform Description

    It is designed to operate in hazardous, confined, or otherwise inaccessible environments. The use of simple 3D-printed components allows for easy field assembly and repair, making it a versatile solution for applications ranging from search-and-rescue, last-mile delivery, surveying civil structures, and military demining.

Our Approach: A Three-Stage Validation of Dual-Mode Mobility

  1. Prototype Development
    • Goal: Establish system integration feasibility
    • Design and build a dual-mode physical platform
    • Enable easy field assembly and repair (3D-printed components)
  2. Ground Autonomy
    • Goal: Demonstrate autonomous navigation
    • Implement Model Predictive Control (MPC) for driving
    • Validate performance in both simulations and real-world tests
  3. Flight Demonstration
    • Goal: Evaluate feasibility of aerial operation
    • Test flight stability via tethered joystick control
    • Assess mechanical and control readiness for flight
