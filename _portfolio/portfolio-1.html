Platform Description

Project Goal

The L.I.F.T. (Land and Integrated Flight Transition) Platform is a hybrid robotic system built to overcome the limitations of conventional single-mode robots in hazardous and inaccessible environments. The platform is engineered for both aerial and ground mobility, offering cost-effective and field-ready deployment.

What It Can Do

  • Provides dual-mode locomotionâ€”rugged ground navigation and quadcopter flight with vertical takeoff and landing.
  • Utilizes a single drivetrain system for both wheels and propellers, reducing system complexity and weight.
  • Features fully 3D-printed modular components for ease of assembly, maintenance, and repair in the field.
  • Supports a range of applications including search-and-rescue, infrastructure inspection, reconnaissance, and last-mile delivery.

Development Process

Step 1: Define Constraints and Objectives

The design process was guided by physical, electrical, and logistical constraints. The platform needed to be compact and lightweight for flight, robust for ground traversal, and feasible to prototype within a 9-month period using accessible tools and materials.

Step 2: Component Selection and Architecture Design

  • Selected the MJ5208 brushless motor to handle both high-torque (driving) and high-speed (flight) demands.
  • Adopted a folding-arm architecture for effective mode switching, ease of fabrication, and compact storage.

Step 3: Structural and Performance Optimization

  • Performed thrust testing with various propeller and voltage configurations to validate flight viability.
  • Implemented FEA-based mass optimization, achieving a 17% weight reduction without compromising strength.

Step 4: System Integration and Testing

  • Integrated key electronics: NVIDIA Jetson board, Arduino Nano, motor controllers, and a centralized power system.
  • Demonstrated autonomous ground navigation via MPC in simulation and physical obstacle trials.
  • Executed tethered flight tests to validate lift capability and basic flight control under constrained conditions.
