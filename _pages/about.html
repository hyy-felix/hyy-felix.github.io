  • There is no moment more exhilarating to me than watching a mechanical system I designed come to life. Throughout my past design experiences, I’ve remained passionate about building robotic vehicles, drones, quadrupedal robots, and humanoid systems. Mechanical design provides the “skeleton,” while control systems breathe “life” into the structure — and I am deeply driven to keep exploring their intersection.
  • I am currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Control of Robotics and Autonomous Systems at the University of California, Berkeley. My focus lies in MPC control development, multimodal mobility platforms, and the integration of embedded systems with mechanical design.
  • In the future, I hope to work alongside world-class engineering teams in industry, contribute to the development of more challenging products, and further strengthen my skills in hardware-software co-design.
  • Every step of my journey has been shaped by the support and inspiration of friends and mentors — each of whom has been a source of growth and motivation. As I move forward, I remain committed to the advancement of integrated autonomous systems and look forward to collaborating with like-minded individuals to push the boundaries of intelligence, reliability, and real-world application in robotic systems.
